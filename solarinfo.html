SOLAR SYSTEM

Our solar system consists of the Sun and everything that orbits around it, including planets, dwarf planets, moons, asteroids, comets, and other small bodies. It formed about 4.6 billion years ago from a giant cloud of gas and dust. The Sun contains 99.86% of the solar system's mass. The four inner planets (Mercury, Venus, Earth, and Mars) are rocky, while the four outer planets (Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune) are gas giants. Beyond Neptune is the Kuiper Belt, a region of icy bodies including the dwarf planet Pluto. Even further out is the Oort Cloud, a theoretical cloud of icy objects thought to be the source of long-period comets. The solar system is vast - light from the Sun takes about 5.5 hours to reach Pluto, yet Pluto is still just a tiny fraction of the way to the Oort Cloud's outer edge. Despite centuries of study, we're still making new discoveries about our cosmic neighborhood, from potentially habitable moons to mysterious distant objects influencing the orbits of far-off bodies.
