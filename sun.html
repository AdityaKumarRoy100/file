SUN

The Sun is the star at the center of our solar system and the source of light and heat that sustains life on Earth. It's a yellow dwarf star, about 4.6 billion years old and expected to live for another 5 billion years. The Sun is so large that about 1.3 million Earths could fit inside it. Its core is extremely hot - about 15 million degrees Celsius - where hydrogen atoms fuse to form helium, releasing enormous amounts of energy. This energy takes thousands of years to reach the Sun's surface and then about 8 minutes to reach Earth. The Sun's surface is covered in features like sunspots (cooler, darker areas) and solar flares (sudden releases of energy). It rotates, but not all at the same speed - the equator rotates faster than the poles. The Sun's atmosphere extends far beyond its visible surface, creating the solar wind that influences space weather. Despite its importance to us, the Sun is an average star in terms of size and brightness compared to other stars in our galaxy.
