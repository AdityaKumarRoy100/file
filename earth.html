EARTH

Earth, our home planet, is the only known world to harbor life. It's the largest and densest of the inner planets, with a diameter of about 12,742 km. Earth's atmosphere is primarily nitrogen and oxygen, crucial for supporting life as we know it. The planet's surface is 71% water, mostly in the form of oceans, which play a vital role in regulating climate and supporting biodiversity. Earth's unique position in the "Goldilocks zone" of our solar system allows for liquid water on its surface. The planet has a layered structure: a solid inner core, liquid outer core, mantle, and crust. The liquid outer core generates Earth's magnetic field, which protects us from harmful solar radiation. Earth's tilt on its axis causes seasons as it orbits the Sun. Our planet has one natural satellite, the Moon, which influences ocean tides and stabilizes Earth's axial tilt. Earth is geologically active, with plate tectonics shaping its surface over millions of years. This dynamic system recycles carbon and other elements, playing a crucial role in long-term climate regulation.
